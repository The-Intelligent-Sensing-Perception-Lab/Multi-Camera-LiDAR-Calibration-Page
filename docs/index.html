Abstract

Overview

Most learning-based camera–LiDAR calibration methods treat each camera–LiDAR pair independently, ignoring the rigid geometric coupling in multi-camera platforms. As a result, per-camera estimates may be individually accurate yet inconsistent at the system level. We present a two-stage framework for joint multi-camera LiDAR extrinsic calibration that combines learned pairwise matching with geometric refinement. First, CMRNext is applied independently to each camera to produce initial extrinsic estimates and dense 2D–3D correspondences. These predictions are then jointly refined through a multi-frame bundle adjustment with reprojection, per-camera prior, and relative-pose prior terms. This approach converts pairwise predictions into a globally consistent multi-camera calibration. Experiments on KITTI (in-domain for CMRNext) and Walkley (Zero-shot out-of-domain) datasets show improved per-camera accuracy and inter-camera consistency.

Methodology

Two-Stage Framework

Our framework converts independent per-camera learning-based predictions into a globally consistent multi-camera calibration in two stages.

1

Learned Single-Camera Initialization

CMRNext is applied independently to each camera–LiDAR pair to predict dense 2D–3D correspondences and an initial per-camera extrinsic estimate T̂L→Ci.

2

Joint Multi-Camera Bundle Adjustment

All extrinsics are jointly refined via multi-frame BA combining a reprojection term, per-camera prior, and relative-pose prior that enforces inter-camera geometric consistency.

★

Robust Refinement Details

A Cauchy robust loss, confidence-weighted residuals, spatial subsampling, and a two-pass fit-gate-refit solver reduce the influence of outlier correspondences.

Optimization Objective

The joint refinement minimizes a three-term objective over all accumulated frames and correspondences:

min  Σi,k ρ(‖wik(ūik − πi(Ti, pk))‖²)  +  λ Σi ‖log(T̂i−1Ti)‖²  +  μ ‖log(T̂12−1T2T1−1)‖²
Reprojection term  +  Per-camera prior  +  Relative-pose prior

Datasets

Evaluation Datasets

We evaluate on two datasets with complementary difficulty profiles.

KITTI Odometry In-domain

Sequence 00 — 4,541 frames of urban driving.

  • Velodyne HDL-64E LiDAR
  • Stereo color cameras (1242 × 375 px)
  • Buildings, roads, vehicles, vegetation
  • Ground-truth extrinsics provided

CMRNext was trained on KITTI-like data, so Stage-1 is already strong here. Stage-2 still improves inter-camera consistency and per-camera accuracy when N=100.

Walkley Zero-shot Out-of-domain

Custom multi-sensor dataset, Ottawa, Ontario, Canada.

  • Two forward-facing color cameras (1920 × 1200 px)
  • Velodyne 128CH-Alpha Prime LiDAR
  • 226 synchronized frames
  • Buildings, vegetation, powerlines, vehicles

Different sensor configuration and resolution than KITTI — Stage-1 predictions are weaker here, making the benefits of joint multi-camera refinement much more pronounced.
